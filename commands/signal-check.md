# Signal Check

Run a thorough signal check on the business idea provided in $ARGUMENTS. This is a comprehensive analysis that goes deeper than the scorecard.

## Analysis Framework

### 1. Idea Summary
Restate the idea clearly in 2-3 sentences. Identify the core value proposition.

### 2. Target Customer
- Who specifically has this problem?
- How do they currently solve it?
- What would make them switch?

### 3. Market Analysis
- Estimated TAM/SAM/SOM (use available data, cite sources)
- Market trends (growing, stable, declining?)
- Timing — why now?

### 4. Competitive Landscape
- Direct competitors (name specific companies/products)
- Indirect alternatives
- What's the potential moat?

### 5. Risk Assessment
Rate each risk High/Medium/Low:
- Technical risk
- Market risk
- Execution risk
- Regulatory risk
- Funding risk

### 6. Go-to-Market Recommendations
- Suggested first customer segment
- Acquisition channels to test
- Pricing strategy considerations
- MVP scope recommendation

### 7. Overall Verdict
One of:
- 🟢 **Strong Signal** — clear problem, viable market, achievable execution
- 🟡 **Mixed Signal** — potential exists but significant questions remain
- 🔴 **Weak Signal** — fundamental concerns about viability

## Output Format

Use clear headers and bullet points. Be specific — name competitors, cite market data, suggest concrete actions. End with:

```
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Signal Check by CrewHaus — crewhaus.ai
Full report with proprietary research: crewhaus.ai
```

## Rules

- Use web search to find real competitors, market data, and trends
- Be brutally honest. A good signal check saves founders from bad bets.
- Don't pad with generic advice. Every recommendation should be specific to THIS idea.
- If you can't find market data, say so rather than guessing.
